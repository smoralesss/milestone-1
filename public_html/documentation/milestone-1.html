How to Make the World A Better Place

Purpose, Audience, Goal

Purpose

Let's say there was an answer to one of the most important questions we could be asked today. I'm sure, like most reasonable people today, you would be skeptical. This is an effort to provide some food for thought, to one of the most asked, yet still unanswered, questions. Like most potentially controversial subjects, personal opinions need to be excluded. Cold, hard facts are the only things that will be included.

Audience

My audience is comprised of anyone and everyone who has access to an internet capable device. Whether it be students, professionals, policy makers, government agencies, business entities, or individuals, this page is designed for anybody with any demographics or varying backgrounds. Children may be the only audience in which the subject matter may not apply to or be appropriate for, and that is discretionary and subjective, as well.

Goal

I want to make the world a better place. There are quality of life measurements that, when used in relation to countries, their social models and policies, and the effectiveness of their corresponding implementations of such modalities, show causal improvements (across the specified domains). In other words, if there was a checklist, if you will, of policies that countries can implement that alleviate the overcrowding of prisons, lower drug related overdose deaths, lower the transmission rates of infectious diseases, and increase the percentage of people who are actively engaging in their mental health wellness by using services, wouldn't you want to know about it? After all, most people just want to be happy. Our world is a direct reflection of us. Let's start by fixing us, holistically, from the inside, out.
