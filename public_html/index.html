Decriminalization

Decriminalization is the removal of criminal penalties for drug law violations (usually possession for personal use). In practice, decriminalization means that otherwise law-abiding people are no longer arrested, let alone incarcerated, merely for possessing a drug. As of 2014, United States penitentiaries (state prisons) housed roughly 50,000 inmates for low-level drug offenses.
